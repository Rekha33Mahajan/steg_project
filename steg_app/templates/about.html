About Steganography

Steganography is the art and science of concealing information within other non-secret text or data. It involves hiding secret information within an ordinary file or message in such a way that it does not appear to be hidden. The goal is to hide the existence of the information.

Steganography is widely used for various purposes, and it offers several features and applications:

Features of Steganography:

  • Concealment: Information is concealed within other data to avoid detection.
  • Security: Provides a covert way to transmit sensitive information securely.
  • Imperceptibility: Changes made are often imperceptible to the human eye or ear.
  • Robustness: Some steganographic techniques are robust against data loss or compression.

Applications of Steganography:

  • Secure Communication: Transmitting confidential information without raising suspicion.
  • Digital Watermarking: Embedding copyright information or author details in digital media.
  • Covert Channels: Creating covert channels for communication in restricted environments.
  • Authentication: Verifying the authenticity of digital content.
  • Forensics: Investigating and detecting hidden information in digital media.
